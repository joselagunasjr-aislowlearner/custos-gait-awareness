# Limitations

- The three primary scenarios are synthetic and simulated. They demonstrate software behavior, not real-world performance.
- The bundled audio sample is computer-generated and intentionally clean.
- Candidate footfall detection uses a simple energy envelope. Flooring, shoes, pets, multiple occupants, background sound, distance, room acoustics, and assistive devices could change results.
- Relative impact energy is not force, weight, balance, or a validated health measure.
- Room-transition duration is contextual simulated evidence, not a standalone gait measurement.
- Five baseline observations and three sustained observations are product-demo choices, not research-validated thresholds.
- The comparison thresholds are conservative engineering heuristics, not population norms or clinical cutoffs.
- Occupant confidence is supplied by seeded data; the prototype contains no identity system.
- The app has not been tested with real clients, deployed homes, production sensors, or production Rockwell services.
- Live GPT-5.6 behavior requires a separately configured OpenAI API credential and may vary within the model alias. Deterministic guards preserve the app's state and numeric evidence.
- Browser accessibility was checked semantically and responsively, but no external certification has been performed.

The prototype describes gait-change and activity-awareness evidence for human review. It does not establish why a change occurred.
