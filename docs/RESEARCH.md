# Research Basis and Claim Boundaries

The cited work supports the measurability and longitudinal interpretation of gait features. It does **not** validate this prototype, its thresholds, its ambient-sensor arrangement, or any medical use.

## Feature-to-source map

| Prototype feature | Research support | Safe interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Footstep timing and cadence from audio | Altaf, Butko, and Juang describe temporal analysis of footstep sounds and extraction of spatiotemporal gait parameters. | Acoustic timing can carry gait information under studied conditions. |
| Step-time variability | Brach et al. evaluate reliability and validity of temporal and spatial gait-variability measures in community-dwelling older adults. | Variability can be measured, but reliability depends on protocol and sample length. |
| Longitudinal cadence, step time, and variability | The LonGenity study measures age-related longitudinal change across pace, rhythm, and variability domains. | These measures can change over time and are appropriate to display longitudinally. |
| Multiple gait characteristics over repeated visits | The Baltimore Longitudinal Study of Aging analyzes cadence and other gait characteristics in relation to walking-speed change. | Multiple features can be considered together; association does not establish cause. |

## Association, detection, and prediction

- **Measurement:** The prototype derives numerical features from an observation.
- **Association:** Research may report that a gait feature varies with another measured factor in a study population.
- **Gait-change awareness:** The prototype compares a person with their own prior observations.
- **Detection or prediction of a health outcome:** Not implemented, claimed, or supported by this prototype.

The UI and generated summary stop at gait-change awareness and uncertainty. A sustained change means repeated measurements moved together beyond the demo's comparison thresholds. It does not explain cause.
